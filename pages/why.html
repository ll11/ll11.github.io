First of all, is it Safe?

Hydroxychloroquine has been used safely world-wide for over 50 years. It's on WHO Model Lists of Essential Medicines. HCQ would not be on this list if it is unsafe. It's as safe as paracetamol(acetaminophen, aka. Tylenol) and metformin, which are also on that list. Valsartan, even though it's the most prescribed anti-hypertensive drug, is not on that list due to its side-effect. HCQ is even allowed to be used during preganancy. The studies that claimed HCQ can cause heart problem either faked the data (and later retracted) or use dangareous high dosage. Almost all drugs have side-effects, if you take too much you will have problem. For example, if you take too much dihydrogen monoxide, you will be poisoned.

It is known some people who take HCQ for more than 5 years and in high doses are at increased risk of retina damage known as retinopathy. Covid-19 patients usually take HCQ for just a few days, so there's is no risk of retinopathy for them.

In June 2020, NIH halted HCQ clinical trial citing reason: "while there was no harm, the study drug was very unlikely to be beneficial to hospitalized patients with COVID-19". While it is unfortunate they stopped the trial for strange reason, at least they admit HCQ has no harm.

Now we get safety out of the way, let's focus on if it works.

Is it effective?

Study after study show, if combined with azithromycin and/or zinc, HCQ is effective in treating early-stage Covid-19 patients.

The comination is important. Azithromycin and especially zinc work together with HCQ to combat the virus. The University of Minnesota trial failed because it did not use azithromycin or zinc. So did many other studies that claimed HCQ does not work. (What Minnesota study did not found is serious adverse effect, such as QT prolongation, even though it use a fairly high dose, which further prove HCQ is safe)

The word "early" is equally important. There are significantly higher number of studies that show HCQ is effective than the ones that claim it's not. In these positive studies, there is a strong evidence that outcomes are better when HCQ is used early in the disease's progression.

A large country-randomized controlled trial with more than 2 billion (yes, the number is correct) people shows: HCQ treatment group has a 79% lower death rate than the control group.

So, yes, it is effective.
